Billing Groups

CREATE

Creates a new Billing Group within the specified Customer. A billing group defines how billing, invoicing, charges, and fees are organised for one or more Legal Entities.

Purpose

Billing Groups act as logical containers that determine:

  • How billing rules are applied
  • Which Legal Entities/Products participate in a given billing arrangement
  • How charges and usage are aggregated
  • How invoices are grouped

They provide a structural layer between Legal Entities and billing logic, enabling more complex arrangements such as multi-entity billing, consolidated invoicing, or separate billing streams within the same customer.

Lifecycle & Versions

Newly created Billing Groups start with:

  • draft version = 0
  • submitted version = 0
  • approved version = 0

The provided payload populates the draft slot.

If submit=true is provided:

  • The draft data is also copied to the submitted slot.
  • The Billing Group is immediately submitted for evaluation.

If submit is omitted or false:

  • The caller may submit the Billing Group later via the dedicated submit endpoint.

Evaluation may return:

  • Missing configuration
  • Inconsistent or invalid billing relationships
  • Document or data requirements

Once all findings are resolved and re-evaluated, the approved version is updated and used across billing processes.

Validation & Authorisation

  • Requires a valid JWT with the CREATE_BILLING_GROUP permission.
  • The referenced customer must exist.
  • The request body is validated; errors are returned in the response.

Result

Returns the persisted Billing Group as an EntityResponse with a 201 Created status code.

CREATE

Creates a new Contact for the specified Customer. A contact represents an individual associated with the customer or its underlying legal entities, typically used for communication, operational coordination, and compliance-related processes.

Purpose

Contacts provide a structured, versioned record of:

  • Key individuals linked to the customer
  • Their roles, responsibilities, and relationship to the business
  • Points of communication for operational, billing, or compliance workflows

Contacts are an important supporting entity, enabling downstream processes such as onboarding, risk evaluation, customer servicing, and regulatory checks.

Lifecycle & Versions

Newly created contacts follow the standard entity lifecycle:

  • draft, submitted, and approved version numbers all begin at 0
  • The draft copy is populated with the data provided in the request

If submit=true is provided:

  • The draft copy is also written to the submitted slot
  • The contact is immediately submitted for evaluation

If omitted:

  • The contact remains in draft form until explicitly submitted via the submit endpoint

The approved slot is updated only once evaluation completes and the apply workflow has run successfully.

Submission & Evaluation

During evaluation the system may identify:

  • Missing or invalid personal details
  • Required supporting information
  • Policy or compliance checks triggered by the data

Any findings are returned to the caller and must be resolved before the contact can be approved.

Validation & Authorisation

  • Caller must have CREATE_CONTACT permission for the target customer.
  • A valid JWT from the authorisation server must accompany the request.
  • Payload validation errors are returned with structured details.

Result

Returns the newly created Contact wrapped in EntityResponse with a 201 Created response.

CREATE

Creates a new Legal Entity, the core business object representing an organisation, its ownership, operational details, and beneficial parties.

Purpose

A Legal Entity is the foundation of the merchant lifecycle. It provides the authoritative record of:

  • Business identity and structure.
  • Ownership and persons of significant control.
  • Operational characteristics.
  • Relationships to other legal entities (for complex hierarchies).

Typical Use Cases

  • Onboarding a new merchant or partner.
  • Registering a new business entity within an existing group.
  • Defining hierarchical corporate structures.

Submission & Evaluation

After creation, the Legal Entity may be submitted for approval. Submission triggers the evaluation workflow, which may identify:

  • Missing or inconsistent business information.
  • Required supporting documents.
  • Compliance or risk-related questions.

Any findings are returned to the client. Once addressed and re-evaluated, the Legal Entity can be applied, making its data active across all downstream systems.

Related Domains

Legal Entities link to several other resource domains:

  • Products.
  • Trading locations.
  • Bank accounts.

These are managed by their respective APIs but form part of the overall entity lifecycle.
